PARTIES, PROPERTY, AND GRANT OF TENANCY

This section forms part of the residential lease between the landlord, Oak & Lantern
Property Company Ltd, and the tenant, Priya Shah and Daniel Morgan, for 19 Rowan Mews,
Cambridge CB4 1ZX. The tenancy runs from 15 April 2026 to 14 April 2028. The parties agree
that each obligation in this section must be read together with the rent, deposit, notice,
repair, access, and conduct provisions elsewhere in this agreement.

For this topic, the tenant must act reasonably, promptly report issues, protect the property
from avoidable damage, and avoid conduct that interferes with neighbours or building
operations. The landlord must maintain the property in a habitable condition, arrange
repairs that are the landlord's responsibility, keep appropriate records, and give at least
forty eight hours written notice before ordinary non-emergency entry. Emergency access may
occur without prior notice when needed to prevent injury, serious loss, or material damage.

The monthly rent is GBP 2,250 and is due on the fifth calendar day of each month. The
security deposit is GBP 2,596. The required notice period to vacate is 90 days written
notice. The tenant must follow these financial and notice terms even when this page
discusses a practical housekeeping matter, because payment, communication, and access
obligations are continuing terms of the lease.

Specific operating rules for this lease include the following: no pets are allowed except a
registered assistance animal or a small caged pet approved in writing; guests may stay for
up to seven consecutive nights and no more than twenty nights per calendar quarter; and
musical instruments, amplified sound, and garden gatherings must stop by 9:30 pm every day.
A site-specific clause also provides that the tenant must use only conservation-safe
cleaning products on the original timber staircase. These provisions are included because
the property has particular building, neighbourhood, or maintenance requirements that may
not appear in a shorter standard lease.

If a dispute arises about this section, the parties should first review written notices,
inspection photographs, payment records, maintenance reports, and any managing agent emails.
No waiver is created merely because one party delays enforcing a term. Any amendment must be
made in writing and signed or confirmed by both parties through the approved notice channel.
