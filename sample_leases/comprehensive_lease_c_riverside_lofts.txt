PARTIES, PROPERTY, AND GRANT OF TENANCY

This section forms part of the residential lease between the landlord, Riverside Quarter
Residential LLP, and the tenant, Amelia Chen and Noah Brooks, for Unit 1206, Riverside
Lofts, 42 Merchant Walk, Leeds LS1 4PD. The tenancy runs from 1 March 2026 to 28 February
2027. The parties agree that each obligation in this section must be read together with the
rent, deposit, notice, repair, access, and conduct provisions elsewhere in this agreement.

For this topic, the tenant must act reasonably, promptly report issues, protect the property
from avoidable damage, and avoid conduct that interferes with neighbours or building
operations. The landlord must maintain the property in a habitable condition, arrange
repairs that are the landlord's responsibility, keep appropriate records, and give at least
twenty four hours written notice before ordinary non-emergency entry. Emergency access may
occur without prior notice when needed to prevent injury, serious loss, or material damage.

The monthly rent is GBP 1,875 and is due on the first business day of each month. The
security deposit is GBP 2,160. The required notice period to vacate is 60 days written
notice. The tenant must follow these financial and notice terms even when this page
discusses a practical housekeeping matter, because payment, communication, and access
obligations are continuing terms of the lease.

Specific operating rules for this lease include the following: one neutered indoor cat is
permitted after registration with building management; overnight guests may stay for up to
fourteen nights in any rolling sixty day period; and quiet hours run from 10:00 pm to 7:00
am Sunday through Thursday and from 11:00 pm to 8:00 am on Friday and Saturday. A site-
specific clause also provides that the tenant must keep the smart thermostat connected so
heating efficiency reports can be produced. These provisions are included because the
property has particular building, neighbourhood, or maintenance requirements that may not
appear in a shorter standard lease.

If a dispute arises about this section, the parties should first review written notices,
inspection photographs, payment records, maintenance reports, and any managing agent emails.
No waiver is created merely because one party delays enforcing a term. Any amendment must be
made in writing and signed or confirmed by both parties through the approved notice channel.
